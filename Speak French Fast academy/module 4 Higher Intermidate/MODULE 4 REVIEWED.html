🌟 Welcome to Module 4: put your French into practice  🌟

Congratulations 🎉 on making it this far! Now that you have built a solid foundation, this module will challenge and refine your French language skills in an immersive and dynamic way.

Daily Tasks are still the same for tasks 1 and 2. Your audio summary should be between 4 and 8 minutes maximum.  For Task 2, you are no longer reading from lingua .com. You only have to listen to a podcast, study the transcript of the day's podcast from France Podcast, and send another voice note of you reading the text after study (study only the recommended podcast of the day).

There are two tasks in one in Daily Task 3 Speaking and Writing. The Speaking Practice is the usual video presentation with topics prompting you to express your opinion over a given topic and defend it with at least three arguments before you can conclude.

Learning to speak and understand a language also entails reading and writing in the language. You have acquired listening, comprehension, and speaking skills in your previous modules. You also need the written skills as part of the four linguistics proficiency skills.

In task 3, you are to do a video presentation on the topic of the day and write a short composition of 80 words minimum and maximum should not exceed 120 words.

Note: you may choose to write a composition at least twice a week if you can’t keep up on a daily basis. This is to acquire the fourth linguistic competence of learning French.
